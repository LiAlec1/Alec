Components of the Sun

Core

Hydrogen is fused into helium here in the hottest region of the Sun, releasing massive amounts of energy.

Radiative Zone

Energy generated in the core moves through this region over a period of 170,000 years.

Convection Zone

Convection currents of matter can be found throughout this 200,000 km deep layer.

Tachocline

Found between the radiative and convection zones, this boundary represents the point at which the layers of the sun begin to rotate at different speeds depending on their latitude.

Photosphere

This thin layer lies directly above the convection zone and can be seen from Earth.

Chromosphere

This thin layer lies above the photosphere and is greatly affected by the Sun's magnetic forces.

Temperature Minimum

Found at the bottom of the chromosphere, this region is the coolest part of the Sun.

Prominence

These loop-like structures can be found above the chromosphere and are caused by magnetic forces.

Transition Region

This transition layer lies between the chromosphere and corona of the Sun.

Corona

This component of the Sun's atmosphere is known as the crown of the sun because it appears to crown the sun during a solar eclipse.

Solar Flare

These intense releases of energy are caused by magnetic forces.

Sunspot

These regions of the photosphere appear to be dark because they are cooler than the surrounding areas.

Solar Wind

This is a constant flow of particles that is released from the corona in all directions.
